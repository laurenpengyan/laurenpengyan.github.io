History

The Yamato Court

In the early Stone Age, the Japanese archipelago was connected with the Asian continent.People lived by hunting and gathering, using crude stone tools. In the Yayoi period, they started rice cultivation as a main livelihood and formed local groups. In 239, Himiko, the queen of Yamatai, governed about 30 local groups and sent an envoy to the Kingdom of Wei in China.

In the late 3rd century, the Yamato Dynasty, centered around the emperor, started to expand its presence. Massive kofun (tumuli) represents the power it wielded. Afterwards, Prince Shotoku and the Emperor Temmu formed a penal and administrative system based on the Chinese model. The governing center was moved to Asuka, where the Yamato court enjoyed stability. Buddhism also started to flourish in this period.

The Rise of Samurai Warrior

In 710, the government set Japan’s first capital at Heijo-kyo, in Nara.Japan first text,"Nihon-shoki,"'"Udoki,"and "Man’yoshu" were compiled at that time. In 794, the capital was moved into Heian-kyo (now Kyoto). This period is called the"Heian-era ."In this period, due to the suspension of exchange with China, a unique Japanese culture (Kokufu-bunka) took root. Several key works of literatures using hiragana and katakana (both are modified from Chinese Character ‘Kanji’), like "The take of Genji,"or "The Pillow Book, " were came out. While the ruler of central government changed from a regent and a chief adviser to the Emperor to a cloistered emperor, a class started to emerge. Later, the Heishi clan led by Taira no Kiyomori came into power, but was overthrew by Minamoto no Yoritomo, the leader of the Genji clan.

Samurai Government

In 1192, after his defeat over the Heike clan, Minamoto no Yoritomo established the first military shogunate at Kamakura. After his death, the Hojo family that served as regents, seized the power. Rround this time Mongol invasions were repelled twice, in 1274 and in 1281. These repelled invasions weakened the government and brought it to an end.

After the fall of Kamakura government, the Emperor Godaigo came into power, but Takauji, a leader of the Ashikaga clan opposed the Emperor so that the government split into two, one in Kyoto and one in Yoshino. The Ashikaga clan won this dispute and in 1336, Ashikaga Yoshimitsu established the Muromachi shognate. In this period, Noh drama and Kyogen established their style of performance.

In 1467, the Onin War erupted and ushered the age into continued battles among feudal lords and peasantry. Oda Nobunaga destroyed the Muromachi shognate and set out to unify the nation under his rule. Christianity was introduced in Japan under his patronage. Toyotomi Hideyoshi accomplished the unification of the nation. The era from the Onin War to 1568, when Oda Nobunaga entered into Kyoto, is sometimes called ‘the Warring States period'.

After Hideyoshi’s death, Tokugawa Ieyasu seized the power through the victory on the Battle of Sekigahara. He established Tokugawa shognate in 1603. The Edo period refers to the time when the Edo shognate served as the government. In this period, the government employed isolationism, breaking off diplomatic relations with foreign countries except for China and Holland. Christianity was also prohibited.

In 1853, the last years of the Edo period, the Black Ships (Western ships with advanced weaponry) steamed into Japanese port, which put Japan’s isolationism into an end. It also led to the end of Edo shognate. During the period between the Black Ships and the end of Edo shognate is specifically called‘Bakumatsu.’

Modernization

After the end of Tokugawa shognate, Japan aimed at modernization and the Imperial Constitution that notes sovereignty rests with the Emperor was promulgated. In 1895, Sino-Japanese War ended with Japan’s victory that allowed Japan to expand its territory to the Riaodong Peninsula and Taiwan, but the Triple Intervention forced to Japan to return the Riaodong Peninsula. Japan entered into war with "the Rossiiskaya Imperiya" (the Rosso-Japanese War) in 1904 but made peace in the end.

Once the World WarⅠstarted in 1914, Japan joined the war based on the Anglo-Japanese Alliance. After the war, Japan served as one of the permanent members of the League of Nations.

Defeat of War to Today

In 1931, Japan brought about the Manchurian Incident, and occupied Manchuria. The League of Union did not justify the occupation so that in 1933, Japan withdrew from it.

In 1937, Japan opened a war with the republic of China while joining the World War Ⅱbased on the Tripartite Pact concluded with Germany and Italy. In 1941, Japan headed into the battle of Pacific War. However by 1945, atomic bombs were dropped onto both Hiroshima and Nagasaki and Japan was forced to surrender.

After the defeat of the war, Japan promulgated the Constitution of Japan was established for Japan, and is based on the sovereignty of people and pacifism. In the subsequent era of high-growth made Japan as the second biggest economy in the world and its economy vastly improved. In the late 20th, however, Japan suffered from the burst of their 'bubble' economy, which caused prolonged stagnation. Only recently the economy has shown a signs of recovery.

In recent years, several natural disasters and accidents, such as the Great Hanshin Earthquake, the Tokyo subway sarin attack, the Great East Japan Earthquake, and the Fukushima Daichi nuclear disaster Nuclear Power Plant accident, suffered Japan and the sense of a need for risk management is growing among people.
